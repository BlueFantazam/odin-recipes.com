BOSNIAN BAKLAVA

Decription

Bosnian baklava is a rich, sweet pastry made of layers of thin phyllo dough, filled with ground walnuts, and soaked in a fragrant sugar syrup. Unlike other regional versions, Bosnian baklava is known for its subtle lemony syrup and soft, juicy texture that melts in the mouth.

To make Bosnian baklava, start by layering sheets of phyllo dough in a greased baking pan, brushing each sheet with melted butter. After several layers, spread a generous amount of finely ground walnuts mixed with a bit of sugar. Repeat the layering process, alternating dough and nuts, finishing with several more sheets of buttered phyllo. Cut the unbaked baklava into diamonds or squares, then bake at 350°F (175°C) for about 45 minutes or until golden brown. While the baklava bakes, prepare a syrup with sugar, water, and a slice of lemon. Once the syrup thickens slightly, pour it evenly over the hot pastry straight from the oven. Let it sit at room temperature for several hours or overnight to absorb the syrup fully. The result is a decadent, nutty dessert that’s deeply satisfying with every bite.

Ingredients

  • 1 package of phyllo dough (about 500g or 1 lb)
  • 300g (about 3 cups) ground walnuts
  • 200g (1 cup) granulated sugar (for filling)
  • 200g (about ¾ cup) unsalted butter, melted
  • 500g (2 ½ cups) granulated sugar (for syrup)
  • 500ml (2 cups) water
  • 1 slice of lemon

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 175°C (350°F) and lightly grease a rectangular baking pan.
  2. Place one sheet of phyllo dough into the pan and brush with melted butter. Repeat this process with about 6–8 sheets.
  3. Sprinkle a layer of the ground walnut and sugar mixture evenly over the dough.
  4. Add another 5–6 sheets of phyllo dough, brushing each one with butter. Repeat the walnut filling layer.
  5. Continue alternating layers of buttered phyllo and walnut filling until the ingredients are used up, finishing with a top layer of 6–8 buttered sheets.
  6. Using a sharp knife, cut the baklava into diamond or square shapes before baking.
  7. Bake in the preheated oven for 40–50 minutes, or until golden brown and crisp.
  8. While the baklava is baking, combine 500g sugar, 500ml water, and a slice of lemon in a saucepan. Bring to a boil, then simmer for 10–15 minutes until slightly thickened.
  9. As soon as the baklava is out of the oven, slowly pour the hot syrup evenly over it.
  10. Allow the baklava to cool and absorb the syrup for at least 4–6 hours, preferably overnight, before serving.
