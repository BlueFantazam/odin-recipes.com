Begova Čorba (Bey’s Soup)

Description

Begova Čorba, also known as Bey’s Soup, is a traditional Bosnian chicken and okra soup rich in flavor and history. It’s a creamy, comforting dish often served as a starter at special occasions and holidays.

The soup is slow-cooked with chicken, root vegetables, and okra, then thickened with a flour-egg-lemon mixture that gives it a velvety texture. Served hot with a slice of fresh bread, it’s a beloved part of Bosnian culinary heritage.

Ingredients

  • 500g chicken thighs or drumsticks
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 100g dried okra (or fresh if available)
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tbsp flour
  • 1 egg yolk
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 2 tbsp sour cream (optional)

Instructions

  1. Place the chicken in a pot with onion, carrots, celery, bay leaf, salt, and pepper. Cover with water and bring to a boil.
  2. Lower the heat and simmer for about 45 minutes, or until chicken is tender.
  3. Remove the chicken, shred the meat, and return it to the pot.
  4. Add okra and simmer for another 15–20 minutes until softened.
  5. In a small bowl, whisk flour, egg yolk, and lemon juice together. Slowly stir into the soup to thicken it.
  6. Simmer gently for another 5–10 minutes, stirring frequently. Optionally, stir in sour cream for extra richness.
  7. Serve hot with bread.
